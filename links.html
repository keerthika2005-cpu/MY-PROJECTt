About the Galaxy

  • Diameter: About 100,000 to 200,000 light-years across, though recent estimates suggest it could be on the larger end of that range.
  • Number of Stars: Estimated at 100 to 400 billion stars.
  • Structure: It's a barred spiral galaxy with a central bulge, spiral arms, and a surrounding halo of stars and dark matter.
  • Location of Earth: We're located about 27,000 light-years from the galactic center, in the Orion Arm.

Top 3 facts about Galaxy

  1. Galaxies are mostly empty space – Despite containing billions or even trillions of stars, galaxies are incredibly sparse. If the Sun were the size of a watermelon, the next closest star would be over 12,000 miles away.
  2. The Milky Way is on a collision course – Our home galaxy is expected to merge with the neighboring Andromeda galaxy in about 4.5 billion years. The result? A new, massive elliptical galaxy nicknamed “Milkomeda”.
  3. Dark matter holds galaxies together – Galaxies rotate faster than expected based on visible matter alone. This led scientists to theorize the existence of dark matter, an invisible substance that makes up most of a galaxy’s mass.
